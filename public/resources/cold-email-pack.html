Guides: Structuring Prompts & Achieving Best Outcomes

How to Structure Your Prompts for Best Outcomes

To get the most out of these AI prompts, consider the following structure and best practices:

  • Define the AI's Role and Expertise: Clearly state who the AI is (e.g., "You are an expert B2B copywriter specializing in SaaS revenue optimization outreach.") and what its mission is. This sets the tone and focus for the AI's generation.
  • Specify the Offer and Its Value: Provide detailed information about what you are offering, its core promise, target audience, and how it's positioned (e.g., "Service: Free Revenue Leak Audit (Value: $2,500)").
  • Set Clear Writing Guidelines: Include instructions on tone, length, and desired structure. This helps the AI produce content that aligns with your brand and objectives (e.g., "Tone: Expert consultant, data-driven, confident but not arrogant").
  • Outline Personalization Requirements: Crucially, list the specific elements that need to be personalized. This guides the AI in incorporating prospect-specific data effectively (e.g., "Reference their company size/ARR range if available").
  • Provide Comprehensive Lead Information (Variables): Use the provided variable placeholders and populate them with actual prospect data. The more detailed and accurate this information, the better the personalization (e.g., "Name: {{first_name}} {{last_name}}").
  • Define the Desired Output Format: Clearly specify how you want the AI to present its response, often in a structured format like JSON, to make it easy to use (e.g., "Output format: { "subject": "Your subject line here", "email": "Your complete email body here" }").
  • Add Specific Instructions for AI Generation: Tell the AI exactly what you want it to generate (e.g., "Generate a personalized cold email with: 1. Compelling subject line...").

What Kind of Data to Feed In to Get the Best Results

The quality of your output is directly proportional to the quality and relevance of the data you feed into the prompt. Here are the key types of data (in general) and why they are important for personalization prompts:

  • Basic Contact Information: Such as first name, last name, title, company name, industry, and company size. These allow for fundamental personalization, addressing the prospect and their company directly.
  • Company-Specific Intelligence: Including recent news, challenges, funding information, competitive context, recent company news, developments, or LinkedIn activity. This is vital for deep personalization. Recent news, funding rounds, leadership changes, or challenges indicate timely reasons for outreach and allow you to tailor your message to their current situation.
  • Industry and Business Model Specifics: Like industry, SaaS category, business model, product category, services offered, target market, and sales channels. This enables the AI to understand the context of the prospect's business and tailor examples, challenges, and benefits that are highly relevant to their specific industry or business model.
  • Financial/Size Metrics: Such as estimated ARR, annual revenue, monthly revenue, company size, team size, and growth stage. These metrics allow the AI to align the offer and messaging with the prospect's scale and likely pain points or opportunities. For example, a growth-stage SaaS company will have different needs than an early-stage one.
  • Offer Context: Details about the offer type, primary benefit, time sensitivity, offer value, main offer, and any available stories or case studies. Providing this ensures the AI correctly positions your solution and emphasizes the most compelling aspects.
  • Target-Specific Challenges/Pain Points: Explicitly stating known primary or recent challenges. This allows the AI to agitate these problems and position your offer as the solution.

By meticulously preparing and feeding this data into the prompts, you can generate highly personalized, effective cold emails that resonate with your target audience.
